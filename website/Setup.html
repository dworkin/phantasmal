Setting Up a Phantasmal-Based MUD

First you'll need to have Phantasmal installed and working. You can do that with either the bundled version or the regular one, but either way you need a Phantasmal test game up and running, such as the one that comes with it. Look at files with names like "INSTALL" if you haven't already read them all. They're important. The bundled distribution should be easier -- download, unpack, run a script. Use it if this is your first time doing this stuff. It'll be a little harder to upgrade the bundled distribution to new versions of Phantasmal and DGD, though.

Transitions

Phantasmal has only just changed over to having a /usr/game directory. Please bear with us in this time of transition. The /data directory is a holdover from the old way of doing things, and we'll be getting rid of that when we can. In the mean time, do what you can, or alter Phantasmal to suit yourself, or use something else. Phantasmal is getting easier to use, but it's never going to be as easy as Melville. It's never again going to do as little for you, either :-)

Directories

When you're building your MUD, you'd like to change stuff in /data and /usr/game a lot. You'd like to change stuff in /usr/common very little. And you'd like to not change stuff in /usr/System at all, if you can manage. And you should never, never change stuff in /kernel. There's no reason to do it, and you'll break compatibility and security if you try.

The /data directory doesn't have much in it any more, and it'll keep having less and less there. That's intentional. Everything is moving under the /usr directories, because the Kernel Library doesn't give you any file security for stuff in /data.

Administrators and Wizards

Since "wizard" may be a class in your MUD and "Immortal" may be an in-character thing too, we like to call them "Administrators". However, no matter what you call them, you'd like to be able to give them different levels of power. That's where those directories can help you.

An Implementor or Head Admin is probably going to need ultimate cosmic power in your MUD. You'll want to give him or her full access to all directories. To put it in Phantasmal's terms, you'll want to "%grant headadmin / full", which is how an existing head admin awards that power from the command line. If you give somebody that kind of power, the MUD will let them shut down or reboot the MUD. It'll let them create and destroy any object in the game. It'll let them crash the MUD in ways you can't repair. It'll let them delegate that power to anybody they feel like. About the only thing they can't do is alter the Kernel Library or other non-Phantasmal files on your machine. You'd better totally trust somebody before you give them this level of power.

A coder customizing Phantasmal for you (not just your game), or an admin that needs to debug it, will need write access to /usr/common. That will let them change the laws of physics in your MUD, the nature of objects, and the ways time and space are managed. This can cause ugly, ugly bugs and you should be careful who you give this access to. You probably don't want to let people do this to your running MUD.

A high-ranking admin, a coder or a Head Builder will probably need full access to your game, but not necessarily to Phantasmal. That means you have some security against them, but they can still do anything they want to the player-visible bits. You'll want to give them at least write access to /usr/game, and maybe full access. Write access lets them put stuff there, while full access lets them delegate power to it. This will let them change the laws of physics on your MUD to some extent, and write very powerful scripts and functions, but they still can't alter any of the previously-mentioned stuff directly. This is also the access needed to add new rooms and areas to the MUD, and remove existing areas.

A new admin will automatically get full access (including delegation) to their own directory. This lets them build interesting things in isolation, but not to hook them up to your running MUD. A probationary builder needs this level of access, and probably no more. A normal builder will eventually only need this level of access when Phantasmal is more polished.
