Chapter 5: LPC/Library Interface

So far, we've dwelt almost entirely on the LPC language and the constructs within it that DGD supports or encourages. However, to actually do anything in DGD, you need an interface to standard libraries and an interface to outside events.

As of this writing, there are a reasonable variety of libraries (aka MUD libraries or mudlibs) available for DGD, including the Kernel Library, Phantasmal, Melville, GurbaLib, 2.4.5, Inferno, BBLib and other small or obscure libraries. There are also libraries that exist but aren't generally available such as Skotos' libraries and the DGD libraries that support Yahoo Chat. There may be more, unreleased and unknown, waiting in the wings.

Each and every one of these libraries exists on top of another interface. Phantasmal, BBLib and Skotos' libraries all build on the Kernel Library. The Kernel Library, Melville and 2.4.5 all work directly on top of DGD's interface. This section will discuss DGD's direct interface, the lowest level of interface available to a library. The Kernel Library, a very different but very valuable resource, will be discussed in chapter 6.
